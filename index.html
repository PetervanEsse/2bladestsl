Welcome to the blog and hub page of the 2Blades Group in Norwich. We are a translational research site of the 2Blades Foundation and are embedded in The Sainsbury Laboratory. Here we work with our colleagues to advance durable disease resistance solutions to help ensure food security. This page links to research programs of the 2Blades Foundation (see below) as well as blogs and curated literature pages from group members.

Soybean Rust

In the 2Blades Group at The Sainsbury Laboratory (TSL) we aim to understand the basic biology of the obligate biotrophic fungus Phakopsora pachyrhizi, the causal agent of Asian soybean rust (ASR). We are using this knowledge to develop and deploy durable and race-independent resistance in collaboration with industry and the Universidade Federal de Vi√ßosa.

Innate Crop

Following a successful programme identifying cell surface receptors against corn stalk and ear rots, the team now aims to identify and deploy cell surface receptors against Asian soybean rust. Combined with the existing 2Blades ASR programme, the objective is to develop soy plants with durable resistance to Phakopsora pachyrhizi.
